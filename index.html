What happened?

A major fire broke out on June 17, 2026 at the Lineage Logistics cold-storage warehouse in Boyle Heights. Officials ordered a shelter-in-place that day and lifted it on June 19, and Governor Newsom declared a State of Emergency on June 21. The fire has continued to burn and send heavy smoke across the area, with air quality reaching Unhealthy to Very Unhealthy levels under a multi-day SCAQMD smoke advisory affecting surrounding communities. An anhydrous ammonia line inside the building was breached during the fire; LAFD, SCAQMD, and Los Angeles County health officials reported no measurable ammonia in the surrounding community. The cause is under investigation, and the operator has pointed to a third-party rooftop solar contractor.

What does it cost to hire Kitsinian Law Firm for this case?
Nothing upfront. We handle every case on a contingency basis, so you pay no attorney fees unless we recover compensation for you. The initial case review is always free.
Do I have a case if I didn't go to the doctor?
Possibly. Claims from an event like this can include more than medical bills: property damage, smoke or ash cleanup, relocation or lodging costs, lost wages, business interruption, and other out-of-pocket expenses. If you have documented symptoms, an injury claim may also be possible. We'll help you assess your situation during the free review.
Is this a class action or an individual case?
This will likely proceed as a mass tort, meaning each affected person has their own individual claim, but related cases may be coordinated for efficiency. Your potential recovery is based on your specific damages, not divided among a class.
How long do I have to file a claim?
California deadlines to bring a claim can be short, sometimes only a year or two depending on the type of claim and who is responsible. Don't wait: evidence becomes harder to gather and deadlines pass quickly.
Do I have to live in Boyle Heights to qualify?
No. The smoke advisory and shelter-in-place order affected Boyle Heights, East Los Angeles, Maywood, Montebello, Bell, and nearby Los Angeles County communities. If you were affected by the fire in any of these areas, or were there for work, you may have a claim.
What information should I gather before contacting you?
Helpful items include: any shelter-in-place or evacuation notices, photos of smoke, soot, or ash on your home or business, cleanup or relocation receipts, lodging and other out-of-pocket receipts, documentation of missed work, and medical records if you sought care. Don't worry if you don't have all of this; we can help you assemble it.
